Earthquake dynamics

I focus on earthquake source physics and fault strength evolution in relation to a range of lithospheric deformation modes, including earthquakes and episodic aseismic slip events, using numerical models, theoretical analysis and observations from natural earthquakes. I use the advanced open-access software SeisSol for simulating dynamic earthquake ruptures for both crustal and subduction earthquakes.

Finite fault zone deformation

Earthquakes appear in finite damage zones of non-zero thickness evolving nonlinear material behaviors, natural complexities, and multi-physics coupling within and outside of fault zones. I focus on the fault mechanics and rupture dynamics in generalized visco-elasto-plastic materials, including off-fault damage and dynamic shear localization. Our multi-scale and multi-physics computational models based on the thermodynamically compatible continuum mixture theory have been validated for a wide range of geophysical applications.

Seismic and aseismic cycles

I use forward models to study both the long-term megathrust earthquake and slow slip sequences. The numerical simulation is built with Boundary Element Method (BEM) with 5th-order Runge-Kutta in time-domain integration. I consider a wide range of data to constrain the numerical models, including paleoseismological data (e.g., coastal subsidence and marine turbidites), geodetic imaging of slow slip distribution, and non-volcanic tremors.
